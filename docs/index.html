Population Clustering Visualizations

These visualizations show Earth's population distribution through the lens of a gravitational potential field. Each populated area creates a "pull" that decreases with the cube of distance (1/d¬≥), similar to how gravity works but with population density instead of mass.

What you're seeing: The "height" in these maps represents how strongly a location is influenced by nearby populations. Peaks appear where many people live close together (cities, urban corridors), while valleys show remote areas far from population centers.

Data source: NASA GPWv4 population grid (2020), 15 arc-minute resolution

World Population Potential

Global view showing major population centers as peaks. Notice the massive clusters in East Asia, India, Europe, and the Eastern United States.

USA Regional Analysis

United States divided into 6 regions, showing how population influence varies across the country. The Northeast megalopolis creates the strongest field.

3D Surface View

Three-dimensional representation of the potential field. Dramatic peaks show concentrated urban areas, while smooth gradients reveal population corridors.

High Resolution World Map

Detailed global view at full resolution. Zoom in to see individual cities and how their influence spreads across the landscape.
